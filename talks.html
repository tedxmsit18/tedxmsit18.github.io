Sanjay Prakash


Are cities living ? This is what Sanjay puts forth in front of us and started upon. Our cities are living, possessing life-like characteristics. There are capillaries that can be the highways, lifeblood that is the traffic and dead cells that are infrastructure. Like every other living being, urban cities too should have a DNA of their own. A DNA that would allow growth and adaption. Cities need to be planned in a functional way.The major question that arises today is how do we design? all the cities are different but they have functional similarities that branch them together as an unit. The biggest mistake architecturally in the last century was to make a system that would last long hence putting the system in a skeleton that would not be function and just stand still. Delhi’s Rajiv chowk buildings that saw its collapse few years back is a prominent example. He further denotes few innovative ways of designing cities, the trial and error method, participation and collaboration between specialists. He quotes, "We should look up to the future because that's when we plan to adapt and grow." He speaks about his recent project in Jodhpur that has quite well surmited from around 500 years ago to make it water sufficient in between areas of drought and deserts by following techniques of system dynamics building baolis, borewells and stepwells.





Dr. Hotta and Dr. Rohit


“Necessity is the mother of invention but difficulties give way to innovation” Dr. Hotta and Dr. Rohit are the BSF doctors who deal with Jawaans on a daily basis, thus, understanding the root of all their problems, related to medicine and healthcare. “As doctors, the typical hospital setup were all we were acclimatised to, at an early stage. It was when we joined the BSF, that the stones turned”. The doctors speak of the lack of proper medical aid reaching India’s Jawaans. They decided to put an end to thesis issues by combining information and communication technologies. “India is an I.T. giant”, they say. Eureka! They came up with the concept of “TeleMedicine” that renders any patient to get medical aid without having to show up at the doctor’s clinic. The three prerequisites for this were: a computer, an internet connection and an EMR (Electronic Medical Record) software developed by the doctor’s unending initiative! They have truly made all Indians proud with their august act!



What is TEDx?

In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TED has created a program called TEDx. TEDx is a program of local, self-organised events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. Our event is called TEDxMSIT, where x = independently organised TED event. At our TEDxMSIT event, TEDTalks video and live speakers will combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. The TED conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events, including ours, are self-organised.
